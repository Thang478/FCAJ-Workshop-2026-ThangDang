### General Assessment

**1. Working Environment** 
The FCAJ program provides an incredibly dynamic, open, and truly "Cloud-Native" working environment. Despite working mostly in a virtual space and via online platforms, there is a strong sense of connection. Community members are always willing to share resources and discuss optimal system solutions.

**2. Support from Mentors / Team Admin** 
I am deeply impressed and grateful for the accompaniment of the Admin team and AWS experts. While designing the architecture for the MalScan AI project, I received sharp advice on adjusting CloudFront routing flows and optimizing NAT Gateway costs. Mentors do not just solve problems for you; they guide your thinking so you can diagnose and fix errors yourself (e.g., troubleshooting Docker library conflicts on Fargate).

**3. Alignment between Work and Major** 
The Bootcamp project aligns perfectly with my Information Security major. Designing and deploying an AI-integrated SOC Malware Platform not only helped me review network security theories but also allowed me to put that academic knowledge into practice on actual cloud infrastructure.

**4. Opportunities for Learning & Skill Development** 
Over the 12 weeks, the amount of knowledge absorbed was massive. I significantly upgraded my mindset regarding Cloud Architecture (Multi-AZ, Load Balancing), DevSecOps (Containerization), and especially Cloud FinOps (cloud cost optimization). My skills in writing technical documentation and community sharing blogs were also consistently honed.

**5. Culture & Team Spirit** 
The FCAJ culture strongly encourages proactivity and sharing. Within the team, there was smooth coordination between the AI side and the infrastructure side. When system issues occurred during the Live environment deployment, the "tackle the problem together" attitude, rather than blaming, helped the team deliver the project on time.

**6. Policies / Benefits for Interns** 
Being provided with AWS Credits to freely experiment with services (like ECS Fargate, WAF, ALB) is a fantastic privilege. Furthermore, the weekend Workshops and technology trend-sharing events organized by AWS brought immense intangible value to the interns.

### Additional Questions

- **What were you most satisfied with during the internship?** 
The practical exposure. Having to defend the infrastructure architecture before a panel of experts and directly calculating/optimizing every cent on the AWS Pricing Calculator provided the true experience of a real Cloud/DevSecOps Engineer.

- **What do you think the company needs to improve for future interns?** 
The program could organize periodic "Cross-team Sharing" sessions midway through the internship, where different teams briefly present the technical hurdles they face and their solutions, allowing everyone to learn from collective experiences.

- **Would you recommend your friends to intern here? Why?** 
Absolutely. FCAJ is the perfect stepping stone to bridge the gap between university classrooms and rigorous corporate requirements, particularly in the fields of Cloud Computing and Artificial Intelligence.

### Proposals & Desires

- **Do you have any suggestions to improve the internship experience?** 
For AI/ML-integrated projects that are resource-intensive, the program might consider providing a "Best Practices" document or boilerplate configurations for managing dependencies on Docker. This would help students minimize time struggling with environment errors (like TensorFlow/XGBoost conflicts).

- **Do you wish to continue with this program in the future?** 
I strongly desire to continue walking alongside AWS Study Group VN and FCAJ in the future, possibly in a community support role or by participating in more advanced training programs on Cloud Security.

- **Other comments (feel free to share):** 
I would like to express my deepest gratitude to the entire FCAJ organizing committee, Mentors, and Admins for creating an intellectual, professional, and highly inspiring playground for students.
